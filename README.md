### Pharmaceutical Industry: The Pandemic Upside

#### Market Opportunity Analysis

**Vaccine Market Expansion**

**Baseline scenario assumptions**:
- Global population requiring Ebola vaccine: 1B–2B (at-risk populations + healthcare workers + travelers)
- Average vaccine price (cost + margin): $20–50/dose
- 2-dose series: $40–100 per person
- **Ebola vaccine market size**: $40B–200B (depends on coverage assumptions)

**Comparison**: 
- Annual global vaccine market (all vaccines): ~$50B
- Ebola vaccine opportunity: 80–400% of annual vaccine market
- **Implication**: Pharmaceutical companies will reprioritize to Ebola/measles vaccine development; other vaccine programs delayed

**Measles Wobble-Aware Vaccine Market**

- **Affected population** (where wobble escape is meaningful): USA 330M + developed nations 1B + high-risk developing nations 2B = ~3B people
- **Vaccination rate needed**: 50–80% (to contain wobble variants)
- **Dose requirement**: 1.5B–2.4B doses (accounting for 2-dose series)
- **Average price**: $15–30/dose (lower than Ebola due to established manufacturing)
- **Market size**: $22B–72B

**Combined Ebola + Measles Vaccine Market Expansion**: **$62B–272B** (significant market creation)

**Ebola mRNA therapeutic candidates** (monoclonal antibody + mRNA combinations):
- Market opportunity: $10B–30B (treatment of active cases; premium pricing)

**Measles antivirals** (post-exposure prophylaxis, severe case treatment):
- Market opportunity: $1B–5B

5. **Competing vaccines**: If multiple vaccines approved simultaneously, market share dilution; winner-take-most to share-market dynamics
